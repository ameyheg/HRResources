1. Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Policy

Indicative framework based on the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013.

Commonly Included Sections:

1. Policy Statement & Scope: Organisation's commitment to providing a safe workplace, free from sexual harassment. Applicable to all employees, contractors, visitors, and interns at all locations.

2. Definition of Sexual Harassment: As per the Act ‚Äî unwelcome acts including physical contact, demand for sexual favours, making sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, any other unwelcome physical/verbal/non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature.

3. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):
‚Ä¢ Presiding Officer ‚Äî senior woman employee
‚Ä¢ Minimum 2 employee members (committed to women's cause)
‚Ä¢ 1 external member from an NGO or association
‚Ä¢ Committee tenure: commonly 3 years
‚Ä¢ Quorum: minimum 3 members including Presiding Officer

4. Complaint Process: Written complaint within 3 months of incident (extendable by 3 months). ICC to complete inquiry within 90 days. Report to be submitted within 10 days of completion.

5. Inquiry Procedure: Principles of natural justice ‚Äî both parties heard, opportunity to cross-examine, witnesses examined, written findings.

6. Interim Measures: Transfer of complainant/respondent, leave for complainant, restricting respondent's access.

7. Confidentiality: Identity and proceedings are confidential. Breach of confidentiality may attract action.

8. Awareness & Training: Annual awareness sessions, policy dissemination at induction, poster display at workplace.

9. Annual Report: ICC to prepare annual report for the District Officer and organisation's management.

üìåOrganisations with 10+ employees are expected to constitute an ICC. The POSH Act, 2013 is a key reference. Employers commonly also set up awareness programs and display the policy visibly.

This is an indicative framework. Refer to the full text of the Act and consult legal professionals.

2. Code of Conduct

Indicative structure for an employee code of conduct policy.

1. Purpose: Sets expectations for professional behaviour and ethical conduct for all employees.

2. General Conduct: Professionalism, respect, integrity, honesty. Prohibition of bullying, intimidation, and discriminatory behaviour.

3. Attendance & Punctuality: Expected working hours, procedures for reporting absence, consequences of habitual absenteeism.

4. Dress Code: Organisational standards for professional appearance (business formal, smart casual, uniform ‚Äî as applicable).

5. Use of Company Resources: Laptops, phones, internet, email ‚Äî for authorised purposes. Personal use guidelines.

6. Confidentiality & Intellectual Property: Non-disclosure of proprietary information. IP created during employment belongs to the organisation.

7. Conflict of Interest: Employees to disclose any potential conflicts. No competing business or employment without prior approval.

8. Social Media & Public Communication: Guidelines for representing the organisation online. Prior approval for media interviews.

9. Substance Use: Workplace is alcohol/drug-free. Consequences for violations.

10. Reporting Violations: Mechanism for reporting violations (HR, anonymous hotline, etc.). Non-retaliation assurance.

11. Consequences: Progressive discipline ‚Äî verbal warning, written warning, final warning, suspension, termination.

Adapt to your organisation's culture and requirements.

4. Work From Home / Remote Work Policy

Indicative framework for organisations offering remote or hybrid work arrangements.

1. Eligibility: Which roles/departments are eligible. Probation period restrictions. Minimum tenure requirements.

2. WFH Models: Full-time remote, hybrid (e.g., 3 office + 2 WFH), ad-hoc WFH request basis.

3. Request & Approval: How to request (HRIS/email), approval authority, advance notice required.

4. Working Hours & Availability: Core hours when employee must be available. Expectations for response time. Time zone alignment for distributed teams.

5. Performance & Deliverables: Output-based tracking. Regular check-ins. KRA/KPI alignment.

6. Equipment & Infrastructure: Employer-provided (laptop, peripherals) vs employee-owned. Internet reimbursement. Ergonomic workspace expectations.

7. Data Security: VPN usage. Secure Wi-Fi. No public location for confidential work. Screen lock policy.

8. Communication: Preferred tools (Teams, Slack, Zoom). Expected meeting etiquette (camera on, background). Overcommunication encouraged.

9. Leave & Attendance: WFH is not a substitute for leave. Regular attendance marking required.

10. Revocation: Organisation reserves the right to modify or revoke WFH arrangements based on business needs or individual performance.

WFH policies are highly organisation-specific. This is an indicative framework only.

5. Grievance Redressal Policy

Indicative mechanism for handling employee grievances in a structured manner.

1

Informal Resolution

Employee discusses issue with immediate supervisor. Attempt resolution within 3‚Äì5 working days.

2

Formal Written Complaint

If unresolved, employee submits written grievance to HR or designated grievance officer.

3

Acknowledgement & Investigation

HR acknowledges within 2 working days. Investigation initiated ‚Äî meetings with concerned parties, evidence review.

4

Resolution & Response

Written response within 15 working days. Decision communicated to all concerned parties.

5

Appeal

If unsatisfied, employee may appeal to a Grievance Committee or senior management within 7 working days.

6

Final Decision

Appeal reviewed within 15 days. Decision is communicated and is commonly considered final.

Key Principles: Non-retaliation, confidentiality, impartiality, timely resolution, documentation of all steps.

Adapt the process and timelines to your organisation's context.

6. Anti-Discrimination & Equal Opportunity Policy

Indicative framework for promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion.

1. Policy Statement: Organisation does not discriminate based on race, caste, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, marital status, or any other characteristic protected under applicable law.

2. Scope: Applies to recruitment, hiring, compensation, training, promotions, transfers, and terminations.

3. Equal Pay: Commitment to equal remuneration for equal work regardless of gender (aligned with the Equal Remuneration Act / Code on Wages).

4. Reasonable Accommodation: Providing workplace accommodations for persons with disabilities (ramps, assistive tech, flexible hours).

5. Inclusive Hiring: Diverse interview panels, unbiased job descriptions, outreach to underrepresented groups.

6. Reporting Mechanism: Any employee experiencing or witnessing discrimination may report to HR. Non-retaliation guaranteed.

7. Disciplinary Action: Violations may lead to corrective action up to and including termination.

This framework is indicative. Reference applicable laws and tailor to your organisation.

7. Employee Data Privacy Policy

Indicative framework for handling employee personal data responsibly.

1. Data Collected: Personal details, identity documents, bank information, medical records, performance data, CCTV footage (where applicable).

2. Purpose Limitation: Data collected only for legitimate employment purposes ‚Äî payroll, compliance, benefits, performance management.

3. Consent: Employees informed at the time of collection. Consent obtained for sensitive personal data.

4. Storage & Security: Data stored securely (encrypted systems, access controls). Physical records in locked cabinets.

5. Access Control: Only authorised personnel (HR, payroll, direct managers) access employee data on a need-to-know basis.

6. Retention: Records retained as per organisational policy and applicable statutory requirements (commonly 5‚Äì8 years post-exit).

7. Third-Party Sharing: Data shared with government authorities as required by law (EPF, ESI, tax). Background verification agencies ‚Äî only with consent.

8. Employee Rights: Right to access their data, request corrections, and know how their data is used.

9. Breach Protocol: Procedure for reporting and responding to data breaches.

Note: India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023, introduces additional requirements. Organisations should monitor notifications for implementation rules.

Adapt based on applicable data protection laws and organisational context.

8. Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy

Indicative structure for managing official travel and expense claims.

1. Pre-Approval: All official travel requires prior approval from reporting manager. Air travel over a threshold amount may need additional approval.

2. Booking: Preferred travel desk / corporate booking tool. Economy class for air; AC 2-tier or 3-tier for rail (varies by grade/seniority).

3. Accommodation: Company-approved hotels / guest houses. Per-night limits by city tier (Metro/Tier-1/Tier-2).
