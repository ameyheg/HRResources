HR Glossary ‚Äî 60+ Terms

Quick reference definitions for commonly used HR and statutory terms in India.

Absenteeism
Habitual absence from work without valid reason or notice.
Appraisal
Periodic evaluation of employee performance against set goals and competencies.
Attrition
Reduction in workforce through resignations, retirements, or other voluntary departures. Attrition rate is commonly calculated as: (Separations √∑ Average Headcount) √ó 100.
Background Verification (BGV)
Process of verifying a candidate's employment history, education, criminal records, and references before or after hiring.
Bonus (Statutory)
Under the Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 ‚Äî employers with 20+ employees must pay a minimum bonus of 8.33% of salary (max 20%) to employees earning up to ‚Çπ21,000/month.
CTC (Cost to Company)
Total cost an employer incurs for an employee annually, including base salary, allowances, employer PF/ESI contributions, and other benefits.
Challan
Official form/receipt for statutory payments (e.g., Challan 281 for TDS, EPF challan for PF deposits).
Confirmation
Change of employment status from probation to permanent after satisfactory performance during the probation period.
Contract Labour
Workers employed through a contractor for specific tasks or durations. Governed by the Contract Labour (Regulation & Abolition) Act, 1970.
DA (Dearness Allowance)
Cost-of-living adjustment allowance, commonly applied to government and public sector employees. Revised periodically based on consumer price index.
Domestic Inquiry
Formal internal inquiry conducted before disciplinary action against an employee for misconduct.
ECR (Electronic Challan-cum-Return)
Monthly return filed on the EPFO Unified Portal for EPF contributions.
EL (Earned Leave)
Leave accrued based on days worked. Also called Privilege Leave. Encashable and commonly carries forward.
EPS (Employee Pension Scheme)
Pension scheme under EPFO. 8.33% of employer's EPF contribution goes to EPS. Pension available after 10 years of service.
ESI (Employee State Insurance)
Social security scheme for employees earning ‚â§ ‚Çπ21,000/month. Covers medical, disability, maternity, and dependants' benefits.
F&F (Full & Final Settlement)
Final payment to an employee upon separation, covering unpaid salary, leave encashment, bonuses, gratuity, and any recoveries.
Fixed-Term Employment
Employment for a specified duration with defined start and end dates, with same benefits as permanent employees during the term.
Form 16
TDS certificate issued by employer to employee showing salary details and tax deducted. Used for filing income tax returns.
Gratuity
Lump-sum payment to employees who have completed 5+ years of continuous service. Formula: (Last drawn salary √ó 15 √ó years of service) √∑ 26. Tax-exempt up to ‚Çπ20 lakhs.
Gross Salary
Total salary before deductions (PF, ESI, PT, TDS). Includes basic pay + all allowances.
HRA (House Rent Allowance)
Allowance for rental accommodation. Tax exemption available under Section 10(13A) based on rent paid, salary, and city of residence.
HRIS (HR Information System)
Software for managing HR data ‚Äî employee records, payroll, attendance, leave, compliance, and analytics.
ICC (Internal Complaints Committee)
Committee constituted under the POSH Act, 2013 for handling sexual harassment complaints. Required for organisations with 10+ employees.
Increment
Annual salary increase, commonly based on performance rating, inflation adjustment, and market benchmarking.
Industrial Dispute
Conflict between employer and employees/union regarding employment conditions, covered under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (now Code on Industrial Relations, 2020).
KRA (Key Result Area)
Major responsibility areas that an employee contributes to. Used as the basis for performance evaluation.
KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
Measurable metric that tracks performance against a KRA. Should be specific, measurable, and time-bound.
LWF (Labour Welfare Fund)
State-mandated fund with contributions from employer, employee, and sometimes the government for worker welfare activities.
Maternity Benefit
Under the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (amended 2017): 26 weeks of paid leave for first two children, 12 weeks for subsequent; cr√®che facility for 50+ employees.
Minimum Wages
Floor wage rates notified by Central and State governments under the Minimum Wages Act, 1948 (now Code on Wages, 2019). Vary by state, industry, and skill level.
Misconduct
Employee behaviour that violates organisational rules or expected standards of conduct. Grounds for disciplinary action after due inquiry.
Net Salary (Take-Home)
Salary received after all deductions ‚Äî gross salary minus PF, ESI, PT, TDS, and other deductions.
Notice Period
Duration between submission of resignation and last working day. As per employment contract, commonly 1‚Äì3 months.
NPS (National Pension System)
Voluntary retirement savings scheme. Employer can contribute up to 10% of salary with tax benefits under Section 80CCD.
Onboarding
Structured process of integrating a new employee into the organisation ‚Äî documentation, orientation, training, and team introduction.
Overtime
Hours worked beyond standard working hours. Under the Factories Act, overtime is typically compensated at twice the normal rate.
PAN (Permanent Account Number)
10-digit alphanumeric identifier for tax purposes. Required for all salaried employees.
PF (Provident Fund / EPF)
Retirement savings scheme where employee and employer each contribute 12% of basic salary + DA. Managed by EPFO.
PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)
Structured plan for underperforming employees, defining improvement areas, targets, timelines, support, and consequences.
POSH
Prevention of Sexual Harassment at Workplace ‚Äî refers to the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013.
Professional Tax (PT)
State-level tax on employment/profession, capped at ‚Çπ2,500 per annum. Deducted by employer and remitted to the state.
Probation
Initial employment period (commonly 3‚Äì6 months) during which performance is assessed before confirmation.
Retrenchment
Termination of service for economic reasons (not misconduct). Governed by specific provisions including notice, compensation, and government permissions for establishments with 100+ workers.
Relieving Letter
Document issued by the employer confirming that the employee has been relieved of duties after completing all formalities.
SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
Documented step-by-step process for performing a specific task consistently.
Standing Orders
Rules of employment governing conditions of work ‚Äî certified under the Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946. Required for establishments with 100+ workers (50+ in some states).
Superannuation
Employer-funded retirement benefit, in addition to PF and gratuity. An additional contribution commonly between 5‚Äì15% of basic salary.
TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number)
10-digit alphanumeric number required for all entities deducting TDS. Used for filing TDS returns and challans.
TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
Income tax deducted by the employer from employee salary based on declared investments and applicable tax slab.
UAN (Universal Account Number)
Unique 12-digit number assigned to each EPF member. Remains the same throughout career; member IDs map to it.
Variable Pay
Performance-linked compensation component paid periodically (quarterly/annually) based on individual and/or company performance.
Wage Register
Statutory register maintaining records of wages paid to employees. Required under the Payment of Wages Act.
Whistleblower Policy
Mechanism for employees to report unethical conduct, fraud, or policy violations without fear of retaliation.
Workmen
Under the Industrial Disputes Act ‚Äî persons employed in manual, unskilled, skilled, technical, operational, clerical, or supervisory roles (with salary ‚â§ ‚Çπ10,000/month ‚Äî threshold varies by interpretation and new codes).

How to Become an HR Business Partner

A comprehensive roadmap ‚Äî from understanding the role to building competencies, transitioning from generalist to HRBP, and proving business impact.

üìã What Is an HRBP?

üí°An HR Business Partner (HRBP) is an HR professional who is embedded within a business unit and works strategically with business leaders to align people practices with business goals. Unlike traditional HR (which is process-focused), an HRBP is outcome-focused ‚Äî advising on talent strategy, organisational design, change management, and performance.
üìåFor SMEs & MSMEs: Most Indian small and mid-sized companies don't have a formal "HRBP" title ‚Äî and that's fine. The HRBP mindset (thinking strategically about people, connecting HR actions to business outcomes, advising leaders proactively) can be adopted by any HR professional regardless of title, team size, or organisational structure.
Traditional HR HRBP
Processes policies and requests Advises leaders on people strategy
Reactive ‚Äî responds to issues Proactive ‚Äî anticipates needs
Focuses on compliance Focuses on business impact
Reports to HR Head only Reports to/partners with business leaders
Measures HR metrics (time-to-hire, etc.) Measures business outcomes (revenue per employee, retention impact, etc.)
