India's New Labour Codes ‚Äî What HR Must Know

India's 4 new labour codes came into effect on 21 November 2025, consolidating 29 older laws. Final central rules expected by 1 April 2026. This section covers the key changes, their HR impact, and what you should be doing now.

‚ö†Ô∏èTransitional Period: While the codes are officially in force, final state-level rules are still being notified. Until your state notifies its rules, the existing old acts continue to apply. Monitor your state gazette for notifications. Last updated: February 2026.

üìã The 4 Codes at a Glance

New Code Year Replaces Key Focus
Code on Wages 2019 Payment of Wages Act, Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, Equal Remuneration Act Universal minimum wages, wage definition, bonus, equal pay
Industrial Relations Code 2020 Industrial Disputes Act, Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act Strikes & lockouts, retrenchment, fixed-term employment, standing orders
Code on Social Security 2020 EPF & MP Act, ESI Act, Maternity Benefit Act, Payment of Gratuity Act, Employee's Compensation Act, Unorganised Workers' Social Security Act, & others PF, ESI, gratuity, maternity, gig worker coverage
OSH Code 2020 Factories Act, Mines Act, Contract Labour Act, Building & Other Construction Workers Act, & others Workplace safety, working conditions, contract workers, welfare

üîÑ Key Changes That Affect HR Daily

Change Old Position New Position HR Impact
Wage Definition No uniform definition across laws Basic + DA + Retaining Allowance must be ‚â• 50% of total remuneration üî¥ Major payroll restructuring needed. PF, gratuity, bonus contributions will increase if current basic is below 50%

The Problem: Many Indian companies structure CTC with a low basic (30‚Äì40% of gross) to reduce PF/gratuity outgo. Under the new Wage Code, basic + DA must be at least 50% of total remuneration. This changes the math significantly.
