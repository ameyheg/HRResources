1. Recruitment & Selection

Indicative reference for structuring the recruitment process.

üìã Recruitment Process Flow

1

Manpower Requisition

Department raises requisition with justification (replacement / new position). Approved by department head and HR.

2

Draft JD with role, responsibilities, qualifications, experience, skills. Post on job portals, social media, internal job board, employee referrals.

3

Screening & Shortlisting

Resume screening against JD criteria. Initial phone/video screening. Shortlist for interviews.

4

Interviews

Round 1: Technical/functional assessment. Round 2: Manager/team interview. Round 3: HR/culture fit. Panel interviews for senior roles.

5

Assessment & Reference Check

Skills test, psychometric assessment, or case study (as applicable). Reference checks with previous employers.

6

Offer & Negotiation

Verbal offer, followed by formal offer letter. Compensation discussion. Joining date confirmation.

2. Onboarding

Comprehensive checklist for structured employee onboarding.

üìã Pre-Joining (Before Day 1)

  • Issue offer letter / appointment letter
  • Collect signed acceptance
  • Share joining date, location, reporting manager, and point of contact
  • Send pre-joining document checklist (ID proofs, certificates, photos, bank details)
  • Initiate background verification (if applicable)
  • Prepare workstation ‚Äî desk, chair, laptop, peripherals
  • Set up IT access ‚Äî email, HRIS, project tools, internal portals
  • Prepare welcome kit ‚Äî ID card, handbook, stationery, branded items
  • Inform team and relevant departments
  • Schedule Day 1 agenda and orientation calendar

üìù Day 1 ‚Äî Joining Formalities

  • Welcome and reception by HR / reporting manager
  • Collect joining documents ‚Äî originals for verification, copies for file
  • Complete statutory forms: EPF Form 11, Form 2 (nomination), ESI declaration
  • Collect PAN, Aadhaar, bank account details for payroll setup
  • Issue employee ID card and access credentials
  • Add to HRIS / attendance system
  • Share and obtain acknowledgement: Employee Handbook, Leave Policy, POSH Policy, IT Policy, Code of Conduct
  • Office tour and facility walkthrough

ü§ù Week 1 ‚Äî Integration

  • Introduce to team, key stakeholders, cross-functional contacts
  • Orientation sessions ‚Äî company history, vision, values, org structure
  • Assign onboarding buddy / mentor
  • Walkthrough of tools, workflows, and internal processes
  • Initial meeting with reporting manager ‚Äî role expectations, goals, 30-60-90 plan
  • Add to relevant communication channels (teams, groups, distribution lists)

üìä 30 / 60 / 90 Day Checkpoints

  • Day 30: Check-in with HR ‚Äî settling-in experience, any concerns, document completion status
  • Day 30: Manager feedback ‚Äî initial performance observations
  • Day 60: Progress review ‚Äî alignment with role, training completion, relationship building
  • Day 90: Formal review ‚Äî probation assessment discussion, confirmation recommendation
  • Collect structured onboarding feedback from new hire
  • Verify all statutory registrations completed (UAN created, ESI IP generated)

This checklist is indicative. Adapt to your organisation's scale and processes.

Indicative frameworks for appraisals, KRA/KPI setting, and performance improvement.

Rating Scale (commonly used):
5 ‚Äî Outstanding | 4 ‚Äî Exceeds Expectations | 3 ‚Äî Meets Expectations | 2 ‚Äî Needs Improvement | 1 ‚Äî Unsatisfactory

When to Initiate: After consistent underperformance (usually rating of 2 or below) and after informal coaching attempts have not yielded results.

PIP Duration: Commonly 30‚Äì90 days.

PIP Components:

1. Specific Areas of Concern: Clearly document what is below expectations with evidence.
2. Expected Improvement: Define measurable, achievable targets for the PIP period.
3. Support Provided: Training, mentoring, tools, reduced workload ‚Äî what the organisation will do to help.
4. Review Cadence: Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins with documented progress.
5. Outcome: Successful completion ‚Üí continue employment. Insufficient improvement ‚Üí further action as per policy (may include role change, demotion, or separation).

Key Principles: Document everything. Be specific, not vague. Focus on observable behaviours and measurable outcomes. Ensure employee has a genuine opportunity to improve.

Performance frameworks vary widely. This is an indicative reference only.

5. Exit Process

Detailed reference for managing employee separations ‚Äî voluntary and involuntary.

üîÑ Exit Process Flow

1

Resignation / Separation Initiation

Employee submits written resignation, or separation initiated by the organisation (termination, retrenchment, retirement, contract expiry). Date recorded in HRIS.

2

Acknowledgement & Notice Period

HR acknowledges in writing. Notice period as per appointment terms. Notice buyout option if applicable.

3

Retention Discussion (if applicable)

Manager/HR explores if retainable ‚Äî counter-offer, role change, issue resolution. Document outcome.

4

Exit Interview

Conducted by HR. Feedback on experience, management, culture, reasons for leaving. Data used for retention strategy.

5

Handover & Knowledge Transfer

Employee hands over responsibilities, SOPs, files, project documentation, client relationships.

6

No-Dues Clearance

Clearance from: Reporting Manager, IT (assets), Finance (advances, loans), Admin (ID cards, keys), HR.

7

Full & Final Settlement

Calculate: unpaid salary, leave encashment, bonus (prorated), gratuity (if 5+ years), reimbursements, recoveries.

8

Documentation

Issue: Relieving Letter, Experience Certificate, Form 16, PF transfer/withdrawal details, any pending documents.
